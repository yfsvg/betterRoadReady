# Why?

Every year, millions of American teenagers learning how to drive use an app called "RoadReady", which is a simple app that logs time spent driving, and then packages that time into exportable PDFs that can then be turned into the DMV and then they will be accepted into Segment 2.

The issue is, this app is total buns! It hasn't received a major update in years and it shows in it's design and the amount of bugs it has. Many instances of people losing their time completely (40+ hours of tracked time list, probably due to all of their drive logs being saved locally on the phone and being wiped after a simple app deletion/reinstallment or something), as well as the app interrupting you mid-drive because you wanted to open Spotify to change the music. The average review rating for this app is a solid **2.1/5**, which is surprising because this app is basically federally endorsed, driving schools across the US all suggest that you use it.

# What this app is

Introducing RoadReady for Michigan. It's like RoadReady, except it's aimed to be less buggy, more reliable for time tracking, and more user-friendly (At least, that's the goal, I haven't finished it yet!)

Built in React Native Expo, this is actually my first mobile app so I'll see how it goes. Although this was built quickly for Flavortown Lockin, I'll be working on this far after the event and probably try to release this by the end of the summer hopefully...
